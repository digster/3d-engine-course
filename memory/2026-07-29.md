## The lesson's spine (CLAUDE.md §4: derive before the matrix)

**Show the artifact first:** the orthographic camera makes distant cubes the same size and dolly
inert — the demo's `[P]` toggle proves it live. **Intuition:** pinhole camera; a point twice as far
crosses the near plane at half the height. **Theory:** similar triangles give `x' = n·x/(-z)` —
divide by depth, the whole of perspective. **The obstacle:** a matrix is linear and can't divide, so
it copies `-z` into `w` and defers the divide — where `w` stops being 1, and why clip space and NDC
are distinct. Then FOV/aspect fill `[-1,1]`, depth maps to `[0,1]` (nonlinear), the handedness flip
lives here, and the matrix assembles.

## Resolved the STATE open question

Keeps 2.7's drop-vs-divide honesty; `xyz()` still drops. New LEARNINGS entry on why two names beat

## Verified, not asserted (one harness, all sections pass)

- Similar triangles at `n=1`: `x' = 1/(-z)` gives `1.0, 0.5, 0.2, 0.1` at `z = -1,-2,-5,-10` — 2.7's
  `w=-z` table, now geometry.
- Projection matrix (fovy 60, 16:9, near 1, far 100): `f = √3 = 1.732`, `f/aspect = 0.974`, `A = B =
  -1.0101`. Depth: near→0, far→1 exactly; **nonlinear** (`z=-2 → z_ndc = 0.505`).
- Worked point `(2,1,-10)` → clip `(1.949, 1.732, 9.091, w=10)` → ndc `(0.195, 0.173, 0.909)`, all
  in range. `clip.w = -z`.
- Demo (fovy 55, near 0.3, far 100, viewport 86×48.4 = 16:9): scene fits the 320×180 panel and stays
  **in front** (min `clip.w = 1.74 > near 0.3`) across the whole orbit + dolly range; view-space z in
  `[-15.2, -1.7]` (set the depth-cue window `[-13,-2]`).
- Default-frame probe: world `(-0.76,1.65,-0.25)` → view `(-0.76,1.07,-6.87)` → clip
  `(-0.82,2.06,6.59, w=6.87)` → ndc `(-0.119,0.300,0.959)` — the lesson's Expected Result HUD.
