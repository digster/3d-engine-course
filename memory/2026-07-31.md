## Decisions (STATE left the mesh choice open)

- **Icosahedron, hand-derived** — not a UV sphere, not the OBJ loader (CLAUDE.md puts that at 3.5).
  Reasons: exactly derivable from φ (so "derive, don't decree" holds), tiny (12/20), 5× vertex reuse
  makes indexed geometry concrete, and it isn't axis-aligned so rotation reads richly.
- **Triangle indices, not an edge list** — even though we draw wireframe. It's what a mesh *is*, and
  what 3.x fills / 3.4 culls / Module 4 uploads. Cost: each shared edge drawn twice (60 draws for 30
  edges). Named honestly; evaporates once triangles are filled.
- **`std::span` (non-owning)** — right for `inline constexpr` data, wrong for runtime-loaded meshes,
  which is exactly the pressure that produces Module 5's asset system. Said so in the lesson.
- **`scene_object` separate from `engine::transform`** — a transform is a placement, not a thing;
  Module 5's ECS attaches mesh and transform independently for the same reason.

## The φ derivation (the nicest thing in the lesson)

Three mutually perpendicular rectangles, width 2, height 2*h*, corners = cyclic permutations of
`(0, ±1, ±h)`. Two kinds of edge: within a rectangle (length 2) and between rectangles
(`d² = 2h² − 2h + 2`). **Demanding all 30 edges equal** gives `2h² − 2h + 2 = 4`, i.e. `h² = h + 1` —
the golden ratio's *defining equation*. φ isn't chosen for elegance; it's forced by regularity.
Normalising by `√(1+φ²) = 1.9021131` puts every vertex on the unit sphere; edge length becomes
`2/1.9021131 = 1.0514622`, and the harness measures all 30 edges at exactly that.
