## The lesson's spine

The formula is one line and the student can guess it. So the lesson is about the *context*:
three things must be right and **two of them are silent when wrong** — the fill rule's bias,
the linear-light question, and attributes coming loose from their vertices during reorientation.

## The best thing in the lesson: the bias has a derivable magnitude

Leaving the top-left `-1` in the weights **translates** the attribute field rather than
distorting it, by `1/‖e‖` pixels perpendicular to the opposite edge — the area cancels. So the
bug lives in *small* triangles.

Then the honest part. Sweeping the stripe frequency on one fixed triangle with one fixed
0.088 px error gave wrong-pixel counts of **4, 0, 0, 15, 4, 0**. Nothing about the error changed;
only where the thresholds fell did. That became the lesson's transferable habit — *derive the
magnitude of a sub-pixel error, never look for it* — and it decided the demo's design: the band
count is swept live so the count visibly jumps. A panel showing one impressive fixed number would
have taught that the bug is visible, which is the opposite of true.

## A claim I had to walk back

I expected float-stepped weights to drift visibly and to use that as the case for integer
accumulators. Measured: **4.94e-6 over 4,000 adds = 0.0013 of a colour level.** Invisible. The
honest argument is narrower — integers are exact, reproducible and cost the same — and it is
still sufficient. Recorded in LEARNINGS.md as its own entry, because overselling a real principle
with a symptom the student cannot reproduce teaches them to distrust the principle.

## Verified, not asserted

- Worked example: altitudes 10/40/70 at `w = (0.4,0.3,0.3)` → **37**.
- An attribute that is itself affine (`3x − 2y + 7`) reproduces itself over 3,721 points, worst
  error 3.05e-5.
- Bias sum is **-1 or -2, never 0 or -3**, over all 495,648 non-degenerate triangles in a 9×9
  grid (247,824 each). Proof: the three directed edges' `dy` sum to zero and cannot all be zero.
- Shaded fill covers **exactly** the flat fill's pixels — 0 mismatches over 400 triangles.
- Reversed winding produces **0 differing pixels** over 200 triangles (the attribute-swap fix).
- Colour: centre of an R/G/B triangle is `(156,156,156)` linear vs `(85,85,85)` encoded — a
  factor of **3.66** in emitted light. R/G edge midpoint 188 vs 128, matching Lesson 1.6 exactly.
- Cost: flat 20.2 µs, encoded 57.9 µs, linear **232.0 µs** = 11.2 ns/px = 11.5× the flat fill.

The demo was rendered **headlessly** by `#include`-ing `main.cpp` into a scratch TU (its helpers
are in an anonymous namespace), so the screenshots came from the shipped code rather than a copy.
It printed 156 and 85 — matching the harness — before any of it was written up.

## Caught by looking, not by tooling

`check-page.js` passed while Figure 4's iso-lines sprawled well outside the triangle (the exact
mistake 2.3's demo made) and Figure 1's value label crossed a dashed bar. Fixed by computing the
iso-line endpoints **on the triangle's edges** rather than eyeballing them. Reinforces the
`_template/README.md` §13 warning: the script cannot see composition.

## Decisions

- **`struct vertex` is a correctness device**, not tidiness — it makes "swap coordinates, forget
  colours" unwritable. That bug fires for one winding only.
- **`prepare_fill` does not orient**, deliberately: orientation moves vertices and only the
  caller knows what a vertex carries.
- **`blend_space::encoded` ships**, defaulting off — third time this bargain has been made
  (2.1's `draw_line_naive`, 1.8's naive collision).
- **The encode `pow` is not tabulated.** Measured, bounded (<0.4 levels at 4096 entries),
  written down, deferred. Optimising 232 µs inside a 16.6 ms budget is optimising by reflex.
- **The demo's uv loop is a second copy of the fill loop, on purpose** — pressure that Module 3
  (growing `vertex`) and Module 4 (GPU varyings) resolve. Two copies is not yet evidence.
