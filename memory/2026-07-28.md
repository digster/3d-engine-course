## The lesson's spine

The loveliest inversion in graphics: **a camera is just an object with a `transform`, and the view
matrix is the inverse of its placement.** Structure: the naive "move the world" fix and why it fails
(it smears the camera across every object and doesn't compose) → camera-as-photographer → `view =
inverse(placement)`, so moving the camera left moves the world right *exactly* → the cross product,
introduced here → the camera basis (backward/right/up, every sign derived against −Z-forward) →
inverting a rigid transform cheaply (Rᵀ, no general inverse) → assemble `look_at` + worked example.

## Verified, not asserted (one scratch harness, all sections pass)

- Cross product: `x×y=z` (right-handed), anticommutes (`y×x=−z`), perpendicular to both inputs,
  `|a×b| = |a||b|sinθ = parallelogram area` (8.3066 for the worked pair).
- `look_at(eye=(0,3,8), target=(0,1,0))`: basis `right=(1,0,0)`, `up=(0,0.9701,−0.2425)`,
  `backward=(0,0.2425,0.9701)` — orthonormal, right-handed (`r×u=b`).
- Eye → origin; target → `(0,0,−8.2462)` (straight ahead on −z at distance d). ✓
- Worked point `(0,3,0)` → view `(0, 1.9403, −7.7611)`, matrix == hand-computed dots. ✓
- **`V · world_from_camera == I`** to 1e−4 — the definition of "inverse" confirmed by arithmetic.
- Moving the eye +2 in x (same look dir) → a fixed world point's view-x drops 2. The see-saw, proven.
- Demo scene fits the 320×180 panel at 5 camera angles; view-space z spans ~[−8.9, −5.0] (set the
  depth-brightness window −9..−5).
- Look-straight-up singularity: `cross(up, backward)=0` → degenerate right axis → demo clamps
  elevation to ±83°; the pitfall + a 7.1 (quaternion) foreshadow.

## Decisions

- **Cross product in 2.9, not 3.4** (see above). **No general 4×4 inverse** — a camera is rigid, so
  `look_at` writes `Rᵀ, −Rᵀeye` directly; the general inverse still waits for a real need.
- **`look_at` lives in `mat4.hpp`** alongside the other matrix factories (translation, affine, and
  2.10's perspective), not in transform.hpp — it takes eye/target/up, not a `transform`.
- **`to_screen3` now projects view space orthographically** — 2.8's oblique hack retired; the real
  camera provides depth. Dolly is a deliberate no-op under ortho, HUD-labelled; 2.10 makes it matter.
- **View rebuilt from the eye every frame**, never accumulated (same anti-drift discipline as 2.8's
  model matrices).
