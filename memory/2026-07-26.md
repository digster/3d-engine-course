## The lesson's spine

STATE's four questions, used verbatim as the section structure, and they carried the whole lesson:

1. **Why 1?** Because `w` is the *multiplier on the translation column* — measured, `w` of
   0/0.5/1/2 applies none/half/one/two of the offset. 1 is forced, not chosen.
2. **Why 0 for directions?** Because translation must not move a direction. And the error when you
   get it wrong **equals the translation**: 10.77 → 107.70 → 1077.03 as the object moves ×1/×10/×100.
3. **Does `w` survive?** The bottom row `(0,0,0,1)` gives `w_out = w_in` exactly, and the product of
   two such matrices has it too — verified exactly after 40 compositions.
4. **Can `w` be something else?** Bottom row `(0,0,−1,0)` → `w = −z` → `x/w` = 1.0, 0.5, 0.2, 0.1 at
   z = −1, −2, −5, −10. That is perspective. Named the perspective divide and handed it to 2.10.

## Best thing in the lesson

**Not one line of arithmetic changed.** `operator*(mat4, vec4)` is byte for byte 2.6's function.
The fix for "my 4×4 does not translate" was entirely in what the *caller* said about the data. That
became the lesson's closing habit and a LEARNINGS entry: when machinery looks incomplete, check
whether it is missing an *input* rather than a feature.

The payoff lands too: `affine(A,ta) * affine(B,tb) == affine(A*B, A*tb + ta)`, verified — Exercise
2.5.3's hand-carried bookkeeping absorbed into ordinary matrix multiply. Rotation about any pivot is
now one matrix.

## Verified, not asserted (5 sections, all passing)

- `w` of 0/0.5/1/2 applies 0/half/once/twice of the translation.
- Direction through rotate+translate: `(0,1,0)` stays `(0,1,0)`, length 1.00 → 1.00. As a point it
  becomes `(10,1,−4)`, length 10.82. Error scales ×1/×10/×100 with the translation.
- 40 affine compositions → bottom row **exactly** `(0,0,0,1)`; `w` returns exactly 1.0 and 0.0.
- 2.6's failing case: `w = 0` moves 0.0000; `w = 1` moves 1.2000 (requested 1.2).
- Rotate about `(4,1,−2)`: pivot is a fixed point; a point 1 unit away stays `1.000000` units away.

## Decisions

- **Named constructors, not separate types.** `position`/`direction` as distinct types would make
  the bug a compile error, and some engines do it — but it roughly doubles the maths library's
  surface and collapses at the GPU boundary anyway. Exercise 2.7.5 argues the other side honestly.
- **`affine()` alongside `translation() * to_mat4()`** — same matrix; one says what, one says how.
- **`xyz()` keeps dropping `w`.** The perspective divide must arrive in 2.10 under its own name, not
  turn out to have been hiding in an accessor.
- **Both failure modes stay on keys** (`[W]`, `[N]`) — fourth time this bargain has been made.
