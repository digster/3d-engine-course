## The lesson's spine

The last hop, and the honest framing: we've been doing it since 2.9 with **three hand-picked
constants and a minus sign**. Two things wrong — it was *underived* (a course that derives the
perspective divide shouldn't hand-wave its final step) and *homeless* (that same minus has appeared
in five places since 2.5). So: NDC as a "unit screen with no size" → the viewport as the enlarger →
three affine maps derived → **the y-flip derived and homed** → depth range → the struct.

## Verified, not asserted

- Teaching viewport 800×600: `ndc(-1,+1)→(0,0)` top-left pixel; `(+1,-1)→(800,600)`; centre→(400,300);
  worked `(0.5,0.25,0.9)→(600,225,0.9)`; and `+y` up ⇒ *smaller* screen y.
- **The refactor moved nothing:** sweeping an NDC grid through both the old constants and the new
  viewport gives worst diff **0.000e+00**. That is the whole acceptance test for a naming refactor.
- Default probe full chain → `screen(81.78, 75.47, d=0.959)`, inside the viewport, with y *above*
  centre 90 because `ndc.y > 0` — the flip visible in the numbers.
- min/max depth narrowing works (`ndc.z=1` with `max_depth=0.1` → 0.1), the pin-in-front trick.
- Widget checked in-browser: `ndc(-1,+1)→(0,0)`, `(0.5,0.25)→(225,67.5)` (above centre 90).

## Decisions

- **Viewport goes in the engine, not demo-local** — 2.11 is literally its lesson, and it is the
  y-flip's one home. Placed in `src/gfx/` (it's the framebuffer boundary), header-only so no CMake
  churn (same precedent as `math/transform.hpp`).
- **Mirror `SDL_GPUViewport` exactly** so Module 4 copies fields rather than translating. Flagged
  `⚠ VERIFY` in the lesson and actually verified against the header, not memory.
- **Write the flip as `(1 - t_y)`, not `-ndc.y`** — algebraically identical, but it *says* it is
  reversing a fraction instead of just looking negative.
- **Dropped the R/U/B HUD rows** (2.9's feature) rather than cram; the six-space chain is the module's
  climax and deserves the panel.
