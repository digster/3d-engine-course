## The lesson's spine

The real subject is a **discipline, not a formula**: always know which space a coordinate is in.
Structure: model vs world as "workshop vs warehouse" → a coordinate is three floats and a room
(and `w` can't carry the room, because space doesn't change how a vector multiplies) → the
`a_from_b` naming rule → **deriving** `T·R·S` from three sentences → worked vertex by hand →
proving why the wrong order deforms → columns-are-the-frame → the other wrong order (orbit) →
when the order stops mattering (and why that's the danger).

## Verified, not asserted (two scratch harnesses)

- Worked vertex: `(0.5,0.5,-0.5)` model, obj `S=(2,0.5,1) · Ry(90°) · T=(3,1,-4)` → world
  `(2.5,1.25,-5.0)`, matching the single-matrix multiply and Figure 2's numbers, `w` exactly 1.
- Columns == scaled rotation axes; `M == translation·to_mat4(R·S)`; det of linear part = sx·sy·sz.
- `T·R·S` holds corner **90.000°** and |x axis| == sx at every angle; `T·S·R` reaches **157.993°**
  at 45° with |x| sweeping **0.35 … 1.8**; `R·T·S` keeps 90° but orbits (origin `(-4,1,-3)`, same
  5.099 distance as the correct `(3,1,-4)`).
- Uniform-scale post and un-rotated plinth: worst |T·R·S − T·S·R| over 360° = **0.000e+00**
  (bit-identical). This is the "two of three objects look perfect" trap.
- Demo layout: all three objects in all three orders fit the 320×180 left panel at every angle.
- The interactive widget's numbers match the harness exactly (checked at 45° and 90° in-browser).

## Decisions (and why)

- **`parent_from_local`, not `world_from_local`** — Module 5's hierarchy widens "parent" without
  touching a character; naming for the world today would force a rename or a lie. New LEARNINGS entry.
- **`parent_from_local` builds only T·R·S**; the wrong orders live in `main.cpp`'s
  `model_matrix(t, order)` — fifth kept-broken demo (after `draw_line_naive`, unswept Pong,
  `blend_space::encoded`, 2.7's `[W]`/`[N]`). A failure you can summon beats one you describe.
- **Written as three scaled columns**, not `rotation * scale(...)` — same 9-vs-27 floats, but it
  *states* "column k = axis k × size k" instead of leaving it to be re-derived (that's Figure 5).
- **Rebuild from a scalar every frame, never accumulate a matrix** — a running `R = R*delta` drifts
  into a shear (the same artifact as a wrong T·S·R, by a different route).
- **Oblique-z projection is a named stopgap** — real perspective is 2.10; said so on the page.
